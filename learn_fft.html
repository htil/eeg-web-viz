Fast Fourier Transform Without Math

What is an FFT?

In simple terms, a mathematical algoithm that processes and computes data from the original domain(often time or space) to a frequency domain. It is usually used to modify or filter audio waves, images, and videos. FFT's are also used to take difficult functions and simplify them.


Frequency and Amplitude

Why Convert from the Time Domain to Frequency?

In order to answer this question, we have to look at what each domain tells us. In most diagrams, you can see the two different domains.(Time and Frequency). Time Domain- You can see the change in amplitude over time but the frequency is shown as one signal. It only shows the average peaks of the frequency in this domain. Frequency Domain- When we view into the frequency domain we actually see that the signal above is actually composed of three different frequencies. It is easier to modify and analyze the signal when it is broken down into its most basic parts. An analogy you could think of are the ingredients to a cake. It's much easier to understand the properties of a cake by looking at the ingredients within. Knowing that cakes are made from milk and eggs lets you know that they do not stay fresh for long. Let's move to the next diagram for a better example.

In the diagram below, you can see the view of both the time domain and the frequency domain When viewing from the perspective of the time domain you can only visualize one frequency as all of them are combined. It just looks like one line with the averages of the combined frequencies. ​ But when viewing from the perspective of the frequency domain you can see three distinct frequencies of different color and amplitude. ​ FFT changes the domain from time to frequency allowing us to dissect the multiple different frequencies that make up each signal.
